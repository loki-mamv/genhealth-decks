Definitions Matter

What IS an AI agent?

Not a chatbot. Not RPA. An AI agent can reason, act across systems, and handle multi-step workflows autonomously.

Chatbot

Answers questions. Waits for input. No action.

× Can't do work

RPA / Rules Engine

Follows rigid scripts. Breaks when anything changes.

× Can't reason

AI Agent

Reasons through complex tasks. Operates across fax, phone, portals. Adapts to exceptions.

✓ Thinks + acts

Buyer Beware

Red flags when evaluating AI vendors

These patterns should make you pause before signing.

× Point solutions posing as agents

They automate one narrow task but call it an "AI agent." Real agents work across your workflow.

× Black-box automation

If you can't see how a decision was made, you can't audit it—and you can't defend it.

× Work shifting, not reducing

Tools that move manual work from one person to another aren't solving the problem.

× Can't explain decisions

If the vendor can't walk you through their decision logic, their system probably can't either.

× "AI-powered" rules engines

Marketing says AI, but under the hood it's if/then logic with a chatbot on top. Ask for a live demo with your data.

The Framework

Treat it like hiring a new employee

The same rigor you'd apply to a key hire applies to your first AI agent.

1

Interview

Request a live demo or POC with your actual data. Not a canned demo. Not a slide deck. Real orders, real faxes.

2

Reference Check

Ask for case studies and talk to existing customers. How long did implementation take? What broke?

3

Onboarding

What does implementation actually look like? What do you need to provide? What's the timeline to value?

4

Performance Review

Set metrics at 30 / 60 / 90 days. Throughput, accuracy, time saved, cost reduced. Hold the agent accountable.

Proceed Wisely

Avoiding over-automation

The goal is augmentation, not replacement. Here's how to get the balance right.

Start Narrow

Pick one workflow. Prove it works. Measure everything before expanding.

Measure First

Know your baseline. Track throughput, accuracy, and cost from day one.

Humans in Loop

Keep people on exceptions and edge cases. The agent handles volume; your team handles judgment.

Automate the repetitive. Elevate your people to the complex.

Getting Started

A realistic implementation timeline

From kickoff to go-live in 5 weeks. Here's what it actually looks like.

Weeks 1–2
Discovery

Share your rules and payer guidelines. Walk through your workflow. Send example orders and documents. Provide billing system access.

Weeks 3–4
Configuration

Review decision trees together. Demo walkthrough of the configured agent. Set up fax forwarding / portal access. Agree on go-live criteria.

Week 5+
Live

Go live with gradual rollout. Weekly check-ins. Tune accuracy and expand volume. Add workflows as confidence grows.

Your Checklist

The decision framework

Before you commit to any AI agent, make sure you can answer "yes" to all five.

✓

Does it understand your full workflow?

Not just one step. The full intake-to-billing lifecycle.

✓

Can it operate where your work happens?

Fax, phone, portal, email—not just a dashboard.

✓

Can you audit every decision?

Full transparency into reasoning, not a black box.

✓

Does it integrate with your ERP?

Brightree, NikoHealth, or whatever you run today.

✓

Will it reduce work—not shift it?

Less total labor. Not the same labor in a different place.

If the answer to any of these is "no," keep looking.
