04
Regulatory Landscape

CMS 0057 Changes Everything for Health Plans

CMS finalized sweeping prior authorization reforms effective 2026. Non-compliance risks penalties, audit exposure, and accreditation loss.

Effective January 2026 — Medicare Advantage, Medicaid managed care, CHIP, and ACA marketplace plans must comply.

42 CFR § 422.566(d): All adverse authorization determinations require licensed physician review and sign-off.

⚡ FHIR API Requirements

Standardized electronic PA interfaces using HL7 FHIR APIs. Plans must support Patient Access, Provider Access, and Payer-to-Payer APIs.

⏱ Response Time Mandates

Shortened decision timelines for standard and expedited requests. Urgent PA decisions within 72 hours; standard within 7 calendar days.

📋 Documentation & Transparency

Specific denial reasons with clinical justifications required. Public reporting of PA metrics including approval rates and processing times.

🏛 State-Level Mandates

California SB 1120 requires medical necessity decisions by licensed providers. NCQA standards demand fairness, efficiency, and evidence-based criteria.

The Problem

Health Plan UM Is Breaking Under Pressure

Rising case volumes, tighter regulations, and clinical staff shortages create an unsustainable equation.

📑

Manual Document Review

RNs spend hours reviewing hundreds of pages of clinical records per case. Fax-based intake creates bottlenecks before review even begins.

⏰

Response Time Pressure

CMS 0057 mandates faster turnarounds while volume grows. Current processes can't scale without proportional staff increases.

⚖️

Compliance Exposure

Every denial must include clinical justification and physician sign-off. Inadequate documentation invites audit risk and regulatory penalties.

👩‍⚕️

Clinical Staff Shortage

Qualified UM nurses and physician reviewers are scarce and expensive. Scaling with headcount alone is neither feasible nor sustainable.

🔗

Policy Fragmentation

Medical policies live in PDFs, portals, and tribal knowledge. Inconsistent application leads to variable decisions and appeal vulnerabilities.

📊

Audit & Reporting Gaps

CMS now requires public reporting of PA metrics. Many plans lack the infrastructure to track, measure, and report authorization data systematically.
